Pipeline safety

Tips for Farmers and Ranchers

You know everything about your land. Now learn what's underneath — and how to keep your operation, your family and your crews safe.

Farm work counts as excavation

Under Texas law, you must contact 811 at least two working days before land contouring, root plowing, deep ripping, terracing, grubbing, fencing, road building, clearing, ditching, dozing or any other activity that disturbs soil 16 inches or deeper. The locate service is free — call 811 or submit your work online at texas811.org.

Depth changes over time

Wind and water erosion can leave a pipeline shallower than when it was installed — sometimes much shallower. If a pipeline easement crosses your fields, ask the operator to verify pipeline depth before deep plowing or ripping, even on ground you've worked for years.

Practical tips for landowners

  • Learn where markers are on and near your property, and note the operator's 24-hour number on each one
  • Get a copy of any right-of-way agreement from your county courthouse so you know what's allowed in the easement
  • Don't build structures, plant trees or place permanent equipment on a pipeline right-of-way without talking to the operator first
  • Wait for every operator to respond to your locate request before starting work — if one doesn't respond, call 811 again and file a no-response request
  • Expect transmission operators to want a representative on site when you work close to their line; accommodate them — it protects you
  • If you snag, scrape or dent a line — even if it looks fine — stop and call the operator. Never bury damage
  • Report exposed pipelines (after floods or erosion) and any suspicious activity on the right-of-way to the operator
  • Make sure family members and hired crews know the signs of a leak and to move upwind, away from equipment, before calling 911

911

Suspect a pipeline leak?

Leave the area on foot immediately and warn others. From a safe distance, call 911 and the pipeline company on the marker. Don’t use phones, switches, vehicles or anything that could spark near the leak.
