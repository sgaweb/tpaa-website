Important Links

Trusted resources for safe digging, pipeline maps, regulations and public awareness.

  • Texas811Texas's one-call notification center. Request free utility locates online or by dialing 811, at least two working days before you dig.
  • Call811.comThe national Call Before You Dig program — how 811 works anywhere in the country.
  • Railroad Commission of Texas — Pipeline SafetyThe state regulator for intrastate pipelines in Texas, including a public pipeline map viewer.
  • PHMSA — U.S. DOT Pipeline and Hazardous Materials Safety AdministrationThe federal pipeline safety regulator: rules, data and safety awareness resources.
  • National Pipeline Mapping SystemPublic viewer for transmission pipelines by county, with operator contacts.
  • Common Ground AllianceNational best practices for damage prevention, including state-by-state digging rules.
  • Pipeline101Plain-language education about how pipelines work and how they're kept safe.
  • Pipeline Ag Safety AllianceSafety guidance built for farmers, ranchers and agribusiness.
  • Damage Prevention Council of TexasRegional damage-prevention programs, training and events across Texas.
911

Suspect a pipeline leak?

Leave the area on foot immediately and warn others. From a safe distance, call 911 and the pipeline company on the marker. Don’t use phones, switches, vehicles or anything that could spark near the leak.
