Before you dig

Planning to Dig or Build?

Fences, trees, mailboxes, decks, pools, plowing — if it breaks ground, it starts with a free contact to 811. It's easy, it's free, and it's the law in Texas.

The leading cause of serious pipeline incidents is third-party damage — often by a contractor, landscaper, farmer or do-it-yourselfer who didn't know a line was there. Even a small scrape or dent to a pipeline or its coating can cause a dangerous leak later. Texas law requires you to notify 811 at least two working days (48 hours, excluding weekends and holidays) before you excavate, so buried lines can be located and marked for free.

Comply with Texas law in 4 easy steps

  1. Contact 811

    Call 811 or submit your project online at texas811.org at least two working days before you dig. Describe where and when you plan to work. The service is free.

  2. Wait for the marks

    Utility operators will mark the approximate location of their buried lines with paint, flags or stakes — or confirm your project is clear. Don't start until every operator on your ticket has responded.

  3. Respect the marks

    Each color represents a different type of utility (see the chart below). Keep the marks visible for the length of your project, and talk with the pipeline operator if you have questions about digging near their line.

  4. Dig with care

    Hand-dig carefully within the tolerance zone on either side of a marked line — never use machinery there. If you nick, scrape or dent a line, even slightly, stop and call the operator immediately. Never cover up damage.

Hired a contractor?

Confirm they've contacted 811 before work begins, and don't allow work to start until the lines are marked. Homeowners are safest treating this as non-negotiable.

Know the marking colors

Locators use the national APWA color code to show what's below:

Red — Electric power lines and cables
Yellow — Gas, oil, petroleum and other flammable products
Orange — Communication, cable TV and fiber
Blue — Drinking water
Green — Sewer and drain lines
Purple — Reclaimed water and irrigation
Pink — Temporary survey markings
White — Your proposed excavation area

911

Suspect a pipeline leak?

Leave the area on foot immediately and warn others. From a safe distance, call 911 and the pipeline company on the marker. Don’t use phones, switches, vehicles or anything that could spark near the leak.
